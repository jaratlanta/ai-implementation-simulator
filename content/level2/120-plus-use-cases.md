## Core Insight

In a community banking client engagement, Meaningful AI identified over 120 distinct AI use cases across the organization. This isn't an anomaly — it's a pattern. Every enterprise has 100+ potential AI use cases when you look systematically across all departments, processes, and roles. The discovery of this volume serves multiple purposes: it demonstrates the breadth of AI's applicability, it overwhelms the "where do we start?" objection with abundance, and it provides a comprehensive roadmap that can be prioritized, sequenced, and executed over time.

## Context

Most organizations approach AI with a narrow view: "we need a chatbot for customer service" or "we want to automate our reports." The 120+ Use Cases approach deliberately expands the aperture, surveying every department, every role, and every process for AI potential. This comprehensive discovery was first fully executed with a community banking client and has become a standard element of Meaningful AI's engagement approach. The sheer volume of identified use cases shifts the conversation from "is AI relevant to us?" to "how do we prioritize among all these opportunities?"

## Detail

### Discovery Process

**Department Sweep:** Systematically interview representatives from every department — operations, finance, HR, sales, marketing, compliance, customer service, IT, legal, executive office.

**Role-Level Analysis:** Within each department, examine specific roles and their daily activities. What tasks consume the most time? Where are the pain points? Where is judgment required vs. routine execution?

**Process Mapping:** Map key business processes end-to-end and identify every touchpoint where AI could add value — automation, augmentation, analysis, or decision support.

**Cross-Functional Patterns:** Identify use cases that span departments — data quality issues that affect multiple teams, communication workflows that cross boundaries, reporting needs that roll up from multiple sources.

### Prioritization Framework

With 120+ use cases, prioritization is critical. Meaningful AI uses a multi-dimensional scoring approach:

**Impact:** How much value (time saved, revenue generated, risk reduced) would this use case deliver?

**Feasibility:** How achievable is this use case given current data, technology, and organizational readiness?

**Employee Pain:** How much does the current manual process frustrate the people doing it? (Eliminate Monotony First)

**Speed to Value:** How quickly can this use case be implemented and producing results?

**Strategic Alignment:** How well does this use case align with the organization's strategic priorities?

### Why Volume Matters

The volume itself is a strategic tool. When leadership sees 120+ specific, relevant AI use cases, several things happen:
- The "is AI relevant?" question is permanently answered
- The scope of AI's potential becomes tangible
- Prioritization becomes the conversation, replacing skepticism
- Individual departments see themselves in the list, creating broad buy-in
- A multi-year roadmap emerges naturally from the categorized use cases

## Application Guide

1. **Comprehensive Discovery:** Interview every department and key roles to surface use cases.
2. **No Filter During Discovery:** Capture every potential use case, no matter how small. Filter later.
3. **Categorize:** Group use cases by department, Gear level, and priority dimensions.
4. **Score and Rank:** Apply multi-dimensional scoring to prioritize the list.
5. **Present the Roadmap:** Deliver the full list plus a recommended sequencing plan to leadership.

## Key Takeaways

- The volume shifts the conversation from skepticism to prioritization
