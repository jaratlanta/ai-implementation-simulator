## Core Insight

1:1 Executive Guidance Sessions are structured follow-up engagements offered 3-5 days after an AI Immersion Workshop. When participants leave a group workshop, energy and enthusiasm are high but questions multiply over the following days. Some participants are shy about asking in group settings. Others are advanced users who want to go deeper. The 1:1 sessions capture this post-workshop momentum and convert it into concrete action plans, moving from "that was interesting" to "here's exactly what we're doing next."

## Context

Developed through a major utility company engagement, where a large-scale executive workshop naturally produced follow-up demand. A compliance director at a major utility company exemplified the pattern: she was already using Copilot in compliance functions but "currently lacked a clear roadmap for optimization." She didn't need basic education — she needed strategic guidance on how to translate individual AI skills into team-level impact. The 1:1 format addresses this diversity of post-workshop needs in a way that group sessions can't.

## Detail

### The Four Components

**1. Executive Q&A**
Hype-free, honest answers to questions about AI safety, ROI, governance, and technology trajectory. Many executives won't ask "dumb questions" in front of peers. The 1:1 format creates psychological safety for frank conversation.

**2. Use Case Elaboration**
Move from vague AI ideas ("we should do something with our data") to defined business cases with specific inputs, outputs, assumptions, and value drivers. This is where workshop energy becomes actionable specificity.

**3. Implementation Roadmap**
For use cases that emerged during the workshop, define ownership, milestones, governance structures, and success criteria. Transform enthusiasm into a plan with names, dates, and deliverables.

**4. Team Enablement Guidance**
The critical bridge between individual AI literacy and team-level impact. How does a VP who now understands AI tools translate that into their team's daily workflows? This includes tool mastery (Copilot, Claude, etc.), practical workflow design, and change management approaches specific to their team's dynamics.

### The Tool Optimization Gap

A key insight from these sessions: many organizations have AI tools available (Copilot licenses, ChatGPT Teams, Claude) but haven't achieved team-level impact. The distinction between having tools and actually getting value from tools is enormous. The guidance sessions specifically address this gap.

### Timing and Format

The 3-5 day delay is deliberate. Immediately after a workshop, participants are still processing. A few days later, they've tried things on their own, hit their first obstacles, formulated specific questions, and are most receptive to targeted guidance. Sessions are typically 45-60 minutes, one-on-one or with small teams of 2-3 from the same department.

## Application Guide

1. **Schedule During Workshop:** Offer 1:1 sign-ups during the workshop itself, while energy is high.
2. **Customize per Participant:** Review each participant's workshop notes and pre-survey data before the session.
3. **Focus on Specifics:** Move quickly past general AI discussion to the participant's specific situation.
4. **Produce Deliverables:** Each session should produce at least one concrete artifact — a roadmap, a use case definition, or a team enablement plan.
5. **Connect to Next Steps:** Use the session to identify whether the participant is ready for deeper engagement.

## Key Takeaways

- 1:1 sessions capture post-workshop momentum that group sessions miss
- Four components: Executive Q&A, Use Case Elaboration, Implementation Roadmap, Team Enablement
- The "tool optimization gap" — having AI tools vs. getting value from them — is the primary challenge
- 3-5 day timing is deliberate: participants need time to try, fail, and formulate questions
- Each session converts workshop enthusiasm into concrete, actionable plans
