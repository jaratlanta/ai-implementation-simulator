## Core Insight

Instead of presenting PowerPoint slides about AI's potential to the board, present a working prototype built on the company's actual data. This is the culmination of the Innovation Sprint methodology — the final deliverable is a live demonstration, not a deck. Board members interact with a real system processing real data, making real recommendations. The difference in impact between "AI could do this for us" (slides) and "AI is doing this for us right now" (working prototype) is the difference between a maybe and a yes.

## Context

Board-level AI investment decisions are typically made based on industry reports, consultant presentations, and generic ROI projections. These abstractions make it easy to defer — "let's study this more" or "let's wait for the technology to mature." A working prototype eliminates abstraction. When the board can see their own customer churn predictions, their own demand forecasts, or their own operational optimizations running in real-time, the decision becomes about timing and scale, not about whether AI works. This approach was refined through a manufacturing client engagement with a strategic advisory partner where Innovation Sprint deliverables were designed specifically for board presentation.

## Detail

### Why Prototypes Beat Presentations

**Concrete vs. Abstract:** Slides describe a future state. Prototypes demonstrate a current capability. Human brains evaluate concrete experience differently than abstract promises.

**Data Credibility:** When the prototype runs on the company's own data, the results have immediate credibility. "This prediction is about your customer" carries infinitely more weight than "AI can predict customer behavior."

**Interaction Creates Ownership:** When board members interact with the prototype — asking questions, exploring scenarios, testing edge cases — they develop psychological ownership of the solution. It stops being "the vendor's tool" and becomes "our system."

**Risk Reduction:** A working prototype is itself proof of feasibility. The board isn't being asked to fund an experiment — they're being asked to scale something that already works.

### Prototype Design Principles for Board Audiences

**Simplicity:** Board members are not technical users. The interface must be intuitive and the value must be self-evident within 60 seconds of interaction.

**Relevance:** Every data point, every prediction, every recommendation must be immediately recognizable as relevant to the company's actual situation.

**Scalability Story:** The prototype should make it obvious what "turning this up to 11" would look like — how this proof of concept becomes an enterprise system.

**One Clear Narrative:** The prototype should tell one compelling story, not demonstrate 10 features. "Here's how AI predicts which customers you're about to lose and what to do about it" is better than a feature tour.

## Application Guide

1. **Design for the Audience:** Build the prototype with board members as the target user — simple, visual, relevant.
2. **Use Real Data:** The prototype must run on the company's actual data for credibility.
3. **Rehearse the Demo:** Practice the demonstration to ensure smooth interaction and clear narrative.
4. **Prepare for Questions:** Anticipate board-level questions (cost, timeline, risk, scale) with concrete answers.
5. **Close with the Ask:** End with a specific, bounded recommendation for what comes next.

## Key Takeaways

- Working prototypes are dramatically more persuasive than presentation slides
- Real data creates immediate credibility with board audiences
- Board interaction with the prototype creates psychological ownership
- The prototype proves feasibility, reducing the perceived risk of investment
- One clear narrative is more effective than a comprehensive feature demonstration
