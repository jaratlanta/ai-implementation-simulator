## Core Insight

AI is driving the cost of creating software toward zero. When custom software can be built in days instead of months, the entire SaaS business model faces existential disruption. Organizations will increasingly recreate expensive SaaS functionality at a fraction of the cost, tailored exactly to their needs. Our technical lead predicts "a whole new GTM model will emerge in the next 18 months" as the industry adjusts. The differentiator shifts from code to expertise: "As the cost to create software converges on $0, we're left to expand our imaginations and subject matter expertise to solve really complex problems."

## Context

The traditional enterprise software ecosystem is built on a fundamental economic assumption: building custom software is prohibitively expensive, therefore organizations must buy standardized solutions and adapt their processes to fit. AI development tools have shattered this assumption. Our team building a CRM in 3 days is a proof point, but the trend extends far beyond individual examples. Companies are realizing they can build bespoke solutions that match their exact workflows, potentially replacing SaaS subscriptions that cost thousands per month. Multiple clients (a steel manufacturing company, an aviation client) are actively exploring this path. The implications for the broader technology industry are profound.

## Detail

### The Economic Shift

**Before AI:** Custom software required large teams, months of development, millions in investment. Only enterprises with massive budgets could justify custom builds. SaaS solved this by amortizing development costs across thousands of customers.

**After AI:** Custom software can be built by small teams in weeks, at costs comparable to a few months of SaaS subscription fees. The amortization advantage of SaaS evaporates. The customization advantage of bespoke solutions becomes dominant.

**The Crossover Point:** We're at or near the point where building a custom solution tailored to your exact needs is cheaper than configuring a generic solution to sort-of work. This crossover will accelerate as AI development tools continue improving.

### The New Value Equation

When code becomes cheap, value shifts to:

**Subject Matter Expertise:** Understanding the business problem deeply enough to design the right solution. The "13 McKinsey experts" capability of multi-agent research systems accelerates this.

**Imagination:** Envisioning solutions that haven't existed before. This is inherently human and becomes the primary differentiator.

**Integration & Orchestration:** Connecting AI-built solutions with existing systems and workflows. The multi-agent orchestrator approach becomes the primary technical challenge.

**Change Management:** Getting humans to adopt and benefit from new tools. The behavioral economics methodology becomes even more critical as the pace of change accelerates.

### Infrastructure Constraints

The one counterforce to SaaS decimation is infrastructure. Energy, cloud computing, and storage are becoming limiting factors. Companies need sufficient compute capacity to run AI systems and build custom software. This creates a potential bottleneck that slows the transition.

### The "Pay for Performance" Future

The team's prediction: as software creation cost approaches zero, business models shift from licensing software to paying for outcomes. Companies won't pay for tools — they'll pay for results. This represents a fundamental restructuring of how technology services are bought and sold.

## Application Guide

1. **Audit SaaS Spend:** Identify which SaaS subscriptions could potentially be replaced by custom solutions.
2. **Evaluate Build vs. Buy:** Use the new economics to reassess every significant software purchase.
3. **Invest in Expertise:** Shift investment from tool licenses to domain expertise and AI capability.
4. **Start Building:** Use Innovation Sprints to prove the custom-build thesis for your highest-spend tools.
5. **Prepare for the Shift:** If you're a SaaS company, prepare for the disruption. If you're a buyer, prepare to capture the savings.

## Key Takeaways

- The cost of creating software is converging on $0, disrupting the SaaS business model
- Custom solutions built in weeks will replace standardized SaaS tools costing thousands per month
- Value shifts from code to expertise, imagination, and change management
- A new GTM model will emerge in the next 18 months as the industry adjusts
- Infrastructure (energy, compute, storage) is the primary limiting factor, not capability
