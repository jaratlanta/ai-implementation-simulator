## Core Insight

The Next Best Action (NBA) model is an ML-powered system that analyzes customer data to recommend the optimal next interaction for each customer at any given moment. Rather than treating all customers the same (blast marketing) or relying on sales rep intuition, the NBA model scores customers on multiple dimensions — purchase propensity, churn risk, cross-sell opportunity, engagement level — and recommends specific actions: which customer to contact, through which channel, with which message, at which time.

## Context

The NBA model was demonstrated in an aviation client engagement as a key proof point for Gear 2/3 AI capability. It represents a significant step beyond basic AI usage — this is AI making real-time, data-driven decisions that directly impact revenue. For many clients, the NBA model is the first time they see AI as a business decision engine rather than a productivity tool. It's particularly effective as an Innovation Sprint deliverable because the results are immediately tangible: "According to your data, these 50 customers are at highest risk of churn, and here's what to do about each one."

## Detail

### How It Works

**Data Integration:** The model ingests data from multiple sources — CRM records, transaction history, communication logs, behavioral data, demographic information, and external signals.

**Multi-Dimensional Scoring:** Each customer is scored across multiple dimensions simultaneously. A single customer might have a high cross-sell score, a medium churn risk, and a low response-to-email score — all of which inform the recommended action.

**Action Recommendation:** Based on the scoring, the model recommends specific actions prioritized by expected impact. "Call Sarah Chen today — she's a high churn risk but also a high upsell opportunity. Lead with the new service tier."

**Continuous Learning:** The model tracks whether recommended actions were taken and what outcomes resulted, continuously improving its predictions based on actual results.

### Scoring Dimensions

- **Purchase Propensity:** How likely is this customer to buy additional products/services?
- **Churn Risk:** How likely is this customer to leave in the next 30/60/90 days?
- **Cross-Sell Opportunity:** Which additional products/services is this customer most likely to value?
- **Channel Preference:** How does this customer prefer to be contacted (email, phone, text, in-person)?
- **Timing Sensitivity:** When is this customer most responsive to outreach?
- **Engagement Level:** How engaged is this customer currently? Rising, stable, or declining?

### Demo Impact

The NBA model is one of Meaningful AI's most powerful demo assets. When clients see their own data analyzed and scored, with specific customer-level recommendations, the abstract promise of AI becomes concrete and urgent. "This model says you're about to lose these 12 customers, and here's what to do about each one" is a compelling argument for AI investment.

## Application Guide

1. **Data Audit:** Assess what customer data is available and accessible.
2. **Define Dimensions:** Select the scoring dimensions most relevant to the client's business.
3. **Build the Model:** Develop the scoring algorithms using the client's historical data.
4. **Generate Recommendations:** Produce actionable, customer-level recommendations.
5. **Implement Feedback Loop:** Track actions taken and outcomes to continuously improve the model.

## Key Takeaways

- NBA models transform customer data into specific, actionable recommendations
- Multi-dimensional scoring captures the full complexity of customer relationships
- The model continuously improves based on actual outcomes
- Powerful demo tool: showing clients their own data analyzed creates urgency
- Bridges the gap between data analytics and operational decision-making
