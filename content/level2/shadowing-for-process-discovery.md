## Core Insight

Before automating any process, Meaningful AI shadows the full process cycle in real-time — watching, questioning, and documenting every step, decision point, workaround, and exception. For an aviation company's month-end close, this meant observing the entire 10-12 business day close process across 7 entities. This immersive approach captures the reality of how work actually gets done (including the undocumented workarounds, judgment calls, and tribal knowledge) rather than relying on process documentation that inevitably misses critical details.

## Context

Process documentation lies. Not intentionally, but inevitably. Official process documents describe the ideal state. Actual processes include shortcuts, workarounds, judgment-based exceptions, and institutional habits that aren't documented anywhere. If you automate based on the documentation, you miss 30-50% of what's actually happening. Shadowing was formalized as a methodology when Meaningful AI discovered that the gap between documented and actual processes was consistently the biggest risk factor in automation projects.

## Detail

### How Shadowing Works

**Full Cycle Observation:** Don't observe a partial process. Shadow the complete cycle from start to finish. For month-end close, that means all 10-12 business days. For a sales process, that means from lead to close. Partial observation misses handoffs, timing dependencies, and end-of-cycle complications.

**Active Questioning:** Shadowing isn't passive observation. Ask "why" at every step. "Why do you do it this way?" often reveals either historical reasons that no longer apply or critical logic that must be preserved in automation.

**Exception Cataloging:** The exceptions are where the real complexity lives. What happens when a vendor sends an invoice in the wrong format? When a reconciliation doesn't balance? When a customer requests a non-standard process? These exceptions determine whether automation succeeds or breaks.

**Tribal Knowledge Capture:** Shadowing is the most effective method for discovering tribal knowledge. The person performing the process often can't articulate what they know — they just do it. Watching them reveals knowledge they don't know they have.

### What Shadowing Produces

**True Process Map:** A map of how work actually happens, not how it's supposed to happen.

**Automation Opportunity Inventory:** Every step classified as: automate immediately, automate with AI judgment, keep human, or eliminate entirely.

**Exception Playbook:** Documentation of every known exception and how it's currently handled.

**Tribal Knowledge Record:** Captured expertise from the process performers that would otherwise be lost if they left.

### Integration with Other Methodologies

Shadowing feeds directly into the Innovation Sprint — the discovery from shadowing defines what gets built during the sprint. It also connects to the Tribal Knowledge Audit, but applied to a specific process rather than a person's overall expertise. And it embodies the Corporate Therapy philosophy: sitting with people, understanding their reality, before proposing solutions.

## Application Guide

1. **Commit to Full Cycles:** Never shadow a partial process. Budget time for the complete cycle.
2. **Ask Why Constantly:** Every step has a reason. Surface the reasons before automating.
3. **Document Exceptions:** Create a comprehensive exception catalog — this is where automation projects usually fail.
4. **Capture Tribal Knowledge:** Record not just what people do but why they make the judgment calls they make.
5. **Classify Every Step:** Tag each step as automate, augment, keep human, or eliminate.

## Key Takeaways

- Process documentation always misses 30-50% of how work actually gets done
- Full cycle observation (not partial) is essential to understand timing dependencies and handoffs
- Active questioning surfaces both outdated practices and critical judgment calls
- Exception handling is where automation projects succeed or fail
- Shadowing is the most effective method for discovering tribal knowledge in context
