## Core Insight

A Tribal Knowledge Audit identifies and catalogs the undocumented human expertise that lives in the heads of key employees — the knowledge that isn't written in any manual, process document, or training guide. This knowledge (what we call the "Key Person Factor") represents both a massive risk (what happens when that person leaves?) and a massive opportunity (what if AI could capture and scale that expertise?). The audit is a prerequisite for building AI systems that genuinely replicate expert-level decision-making.

## Context

Every organization has critical knowledge that exists only in the minds of experienced employees. The sales rep who knows exactly which tone to use with a difficult customer. The operations manager who can predict a supply chain disruption by feel. The finance analyst who spots anomalies in reports that no rule could codify. This tribal knowledge is the organization's most valuable and most fragile asset. The Tribal Knowledge Audit was developed as part of Meaningful AI's discovery process to capture this expertise before it can be lost — and to feed it into AI systems that can scale it across the organization.

## Detail

### What Tribal Knowledge Looks Like

**Decision Heuristics:** "When I see X, I always do Y because Z happened 5 years ago." These rules of thumb are enormously valuable but rarely documented.

**Relationship Intelligence:** Who to call when a deal is stuck, which supplier responds to urgency, which internal stakeholder needs to be consulted before a decision. This network knowledge lives entirely in people's heads.

**Pattern Recognition:** Experienced employees spot patterns that haven't been formalized into rules. A loan officer who can "smell" a bad application. A plant manager who hears a machine running differently. These intuitive recognitions are based on years of experience.

**Process Workarounds:** The unofficial ways that experienced employees get things done — the shortcuts, the exceptions, the "we don't do it by the book because the book is wrong" knowledge that keeps organizations running.

### The Audit Process

**Identify Key Experts:** Map the people whose departure would create the biggest knowledge gaps.

**Deep Interviews:** Conduct structured but conversational interviews (this is where corporate therapy skills apply) to extract knowledge that experts don't even realize they have.

**Scenario Mapping:** Walk experts through specific scenarios and document their decision-making process at each branch point.

**Knowledge Documentation:** Translate captured knowledge into formats that can inform AI system design — decision trees, rules engines, training data, and prompt engineering specifications.

**Validation:** Review documented knowledge with the experts to confirm accuracy and completeness.

### Why This Matters for AI

AI systems can only be as good as the knowledge they're built on. If an AI agent is designed to qualify sales leads but doesn't incorporate the tribal knowledge of top performers, it will make decisions that look reasonable on paper but miss the nuances that separate good deals from bad ones. The Tribal Knowledge Audit ensures that AI systems are built on real expertise, not just documented processes.

## Application Guide

1. **Map Key Experts:** Identify the 10-20 people whose knowledge is most critical and least documented.
2. **Interview Deeply:** Use structured interviews to surface implicit knowledge.
3. **Scenario Test:** Walk through real scenarios to capture decision-making patterns.
4. **Document for AI:** Translate findings into formats usable for AI system design.
5. **Build and Validate:** Create AI systems that embody captured knowledge and validate with experts.

## Key Takeaways

- Tribal knowledge is an organization's most valuable and most fragile asset
- AI systems built without tribal knowledge make decisions that miss critical nuances
- The audit captures decision heuristics, relationship intelligence, pattern recognition, and process workarounds
- Deep, empathetic interviewing is required to surface knowledge experts don't realize they have
- Captured tribal knowledge becomes training data and prompt engineering input for AI systems
